Root canals are among the most common dental procedures performed — more than 15 million every year in the United States alone. The success rate exceeds 95% in most cases.

The success rate for root canal treatment is remarkably high, with studies showing it exceeds 95% in most cases. Modern endodontic techniques and materials have significantly improved outcomes over the past few decades. When performed by a skilled dentist or endodontist, a root canal can save your tooth and eliminate the pain caused by infection.

Factors that influence success include the extent of infection, the skill of the dentist, and how well you care for your tooth afterward. Post-treatment care, including a dental crown, significantly affects the longevity of the treated tooth. When done properly, a root canal can last a lifetime.
